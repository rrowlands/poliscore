The World's First Legislative Rating Service

A Misson to Serve Voters

At Poliscore, our mission is to provide a clear, non-partisan evaluation of legislative performance in America. In an era where misinformation and sensationalized news dominate, we aim to bring clarity and accountability by grading legislators based on their actual legislative actions, not media portrayals. We utilize advanced AI technology to scan and interpret bills, which we then use to create comprehensive performance metrics for legislators. This approach ensures discussions are rooted in verifiable policy actions rather than partisan rhetoric.

Building an AI Judge

How is it possible to grade the performance of legislators in a non-partisan way? Well, all of the bills and legislator interpretations were generated with the same partisan neutral prompt, using the same unmodified AI model. This means that we have one singular, non-partisan, robot judge which is responsible for grading everyone. This robot judge has consumed and, in theory, comprehended the vast majority of the internet and written text. Every opinion or interpretation (with the exclusion of this page) was generated by AI.

The creators of Chat GPT were smart, however, and included within it, natural guard rails to prevent it from being drawn into political debate (their goal being to remain non-partisan). You will see this if you ask Chat GPT, “What is your opinion of Bernie Sanders”? It will either refuse to answer the question, or respond with a wishy-washy “non-answer”. This is fine, because if we take a stance on a particular legislator, we want to be able to back up our stance with data in a traceable way, something which is incredibly hard to do with generative AI (which is known for making things up).

Scoring 12,767 Bills

This is why we decided to start with bill interpretations. For every bill in the 118th congress, AI was asked to “score the bill on the estimated impact to society upon the following criteria”, one of them being “overall benefit to society”. AI was also asked to include an explanation as to why it rated a bill a certain way. This “overall benefit to society” is the metric used to produce a letter grade for a bill or legislator. We then calculated a weighted average across all bills a legislator interacted with, and these scores are then used to grade a legislator. For every legislator we graded, you are able to see every bill we have interpreted, thus making this data traceable.

AI is a Liberal?

When looking through the data, you will notice an interesting trend. AI appears to be rating one political party more favorably than the other. This begs the question: is it possible AI has a secret partisan bias? Or is it the case that the algorithm works and one party more closely represents a more favorable “overall benefit to society”?

Upon closer inspection of the data, it becomes clear that AI consistently takes the following stances:

  • Bills rolling back EPA regulations are consistently scored negatively
  • Bills criminalizing gender affirming care for trans-sexuals are consistently scored negatively
  • Bills affirming the Roe v Wade decision are scored positively
  • Bills promoting deregulation of firearms are scored negatively

It would appear that AI is a liberal? It also sure seems to be talking about social equity quite a bit. We wish to challenge this narrative a little bit. Regarding climate change, almost 70% of Americans want to transition to renewable energy. 62% of Americans are in favor of the Roe v Wade decision. If the majority of the public agrees, is it true that these are really partisan issues? The science on these issues is pretty clear, AI is simply grading them based on the science, and AI has been asked to grade them on what’s best for humanity. Perhaps it’s the case that AI is being non-partisan, and our legislators are simply out of alignment with the will of the people. Have we been tricked into playing a red versus blue game at the expense of humanity? Maybe the only way our country can heal, is not to ask “is this a left or a right issue”, and to instead ask “is this good for our society?” Maybe that's what it really means to be non-partisan.

Specifics on Bill Scoring

The current data loaded on this website is all bills (excluding SCONRES, HCONRES, HRES, SRES) of the 118th US congress. Bill status data and legislator data was fetched using the open source Github USC project.

Bill text was split into sections and interpreted using Open AI's GPT-4o with the following prompt:

You will be given the text of a United States bill currently in congress. Your role is to be a non-partisan oversight committee, evaluating whether or not the following bill will produce a positive overall benefit to society. In your response, fill out the sections as listed in the following template. Each section will have detailed instructions on how to fill it out. Make sure to include the section title (such as, 'Stats:') in your response. Do not include the section instructions in your response.

Stats:
Score the following bill on the estimated impact to the United States upon the following criteria, rated from -100 (very harmful) to 0 (neutral) to +100 (very helpful) or N/A if it is not relevant. If the bill includes riders, please score it negatively.

Agriculture and Food: <score or N/A>
Education: <score or N/A>
Transportation: <score or N/A>
Economics and Commerce: <score or N/A>
Foreign relations: <score or N/A>
Social equity: <score or N/A>
Government Efficiency and Management: <score or N/A>
Healthcare: <score or N/A>
Housing: <score or N/A>
Energy: <score or N/A>
Technology: <score or N/A>
Immigration: <score or N/A>
National Defense: <score or N/A>
Crime and Law Enforcement: <score or N/A>
Wildlife and Forest Management: <score or N/A>
Public Lands and Natural Resources: <score or N/A>
Environmental Management and climate change: <score or N/A>
Overall benefit to society: <score or N/A>

Bill Title:
Write the bill title. If the bill does not have a title and is only referred to by its bill number (such as HR 4141), please make up a very short title for the bill based on its content.

Riders:
- List of bill riders identified and the section they occured at, or 'None' if there are none

Short Report:
A single paragraph, at least four sentence report which gives a detailed, but not repetitive, summary of the bill, any high level goals, and it's expected impact to society. If riders are present in the bill mention them and the section they occur at here, otherwise do not mention their absence. Do not include any formatting text, such as stars or dashes. Do not include non-human readable text such as XML ids.

Long Report:
A detailed, but not repetitive, report of the bill which references concrete, notable and specific text of the bill where possible. Make sure to explain: an overall summary of the bill; the high level goals the bill is attempting to achieve, and how it plans to achieve those goals; the impact to society the bill would have, if enacted. Your audience here is general public layman voters, so if you think they won't understand an acronym or a complex topic, please explain it. Should be between one and seven paragraphs long, depending on the complexity of the bill and the topics it covers. If the bill touches on controversial topics such as trans issues or guns rights, please include the advocating logic by proponents and also the advocating logic of the opposition, otherwise do not include this logic. Where relevant, cite scientific studies or the opinions of authoritative knowledge sources to provide more context. Keep in mind that we're trying to figure out how to spend U.S. taxpayer dollars: budgetary concerns are important. If there are riders in the bill, mention them in this summary, otherwise do not mention their absence. Do not include any formatting text, such as stars or dashes. Do not include non-human readable text such as XML ids.

Specifics on Legislator Scoring

To calculate legislator statistics, we calculated a weighted average across all each legislator's bill interactions. The weights were assigned as follows:

  • Sponsor: 1.0
  • Cosponsor: 0.7
  • Voted For: 0.5
  • Voted Against: -0.5

Votes against a bill were assigned a negative weight, which simply means that if the legislator voted against a bill with a negative score then it affected them positively and vise versa.

Legislator and Bill Letter Grades

Letter grades for both bills and legislators are calculated with the same, simple algorithm. The grades are derived entirely from the 'Overall Benefit to Society' statistic and are assigned as follows:

  • A: greater than or equal to 40
  • B: greater than or equal to 30 and less than 40
  • C: greater than or equal to 15 and less than 30
  • D: greater than or equal to 0 and less than 15
  • F: less than zero

Parameterized Legislator Summaries

Legislator summaries are calculated using a parameterized prompt which includes information from the previously mentioned aggregate scoring algorithm. The prompt is as follows:

The United States {{politicianType}} {{fullName}} has been evaluated based on recent legislative performance and has received the following policy area grades (scores range from -100 to 100):

{{stats}}

Based on these scores, this legislator has received the overall letter grade: {{letterGrade}}. You will be given bill interaction summaries of this politician’s recent legislative history, sorted by their impact to the relevant policy area grades. Please generate a layman's, concise, three paragraph, {{analysisType}}, highlighting any {{behavior}}, identifying trends, referencing specific bill titles (in quotes), and pointing out major focuses and priorities of the legislator. Focus on the policy areas with the largest score magnitudes (either positive or negative). Do not include the legislator's policy area grade scores and do not mention their letter grade in your summary.

{{billInteractions}}

The parameters are replaced as follows:

  • politicianType: Either 'Senator' or 'House Representative', depending on the chamber of their legislative term.
  • fullName: The legislator's full first and last name.
  • stats: The previously calculated legislator's policy score statistics, written as text.
  • letterGrade: The calculated letter grade for the legislator, as described in 'Legislator and Bill Letter Grades'
  • analysisType: One of 'endorsement', 'mixed analysis' or 'harsh critique', dependent upon the legislator's previosuly calculated letter grade. A's and B's are assigned as 'endorsement'. C's and D's are assigned as 'mixed analysis'. F's are assigned as 'harsh critique'.
  • behavior: One of 'specific accomplishments', 'specific accomplishments or alarming behaviour', or 'alarming behaviour', dependent upon the legislator's previously calculated letter grade. A's and B's are assigned as 'specific accomplishments'. C's and D's are assigned as 'specific accomplishments or alarming behaviour'. F's are assigned as 'alarming behaviour'.
  • billInteractions: A list of previously calculated bill summaries of the legislator's bill interactions. The bill interactions are first grouped by the policy areas, and then they are sorted by the absolute value of the score magnitude multiplied by the interaction weight. We then rotate through the policy areas, including the top bills for each policy area until we run out of token space in the prompt.
